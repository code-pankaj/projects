Human Anatomy Models

The human heart is a muscular organ that pumps blood through the blood vessels by repeated, rhythmic contractions. It is located in the thoracic cavity and is composed of four chambers: two atria and two ventricles. The heart plays a crucial role in maintaining the circulatory system, delivering oxygen and nutrients to various parts of the body, while also removing waste products.

The human skeleton consists of 206 bones that provide structure, protect internal organs, and enable movement. The skeleton is divided into two parts: the axial skeleton, which includes the skull, spine, and ribcage, and the appendicular skeleton, which includes the limbs and pelvis. Bones are connected by joints and are crucial for bodily movement and support.

The human muscular system is composed of over 600 muscles that help in movement, posture maintenance, and the generation of body heat. Major muscles include the biceps, quadriceps, triceps, and glutes. Muscles work by contracting and relaxing to produce movement. They can be voluntary or involuntary, depending on the function they serve.

The human brain is the central organ of the human nervous system. It is located in the head and is responsible for controlling various functions, including thoughts, memory, emotions, and coordination. The brain is divided into several regions such as the cerebrum, cerebellum, and brainstem, each of which plays a specific role in processing information and managing bodily functions.
